How do Companies evaluate you with Credit score?

  • If you make payments on time. (Companies utilize your payment history to determine how consistent you are with payments.)
  • If you owe any money. (This is important for financial firms because this is used to determine how much of a liability you may be depending on how much you owe to other firms and whether you are in good standings with the firm.)
  • Make late payments. (A firm will use this to determine if you are delinquent with payments.)
  • If you are responsible with your credit card (This is dependent on the factors mentioned above and it is how your responsibility is tested.)
  • Whether or not you are a risk or liability (Companies use your overall credit score and other factors mentioned to label you either responsible or a liability.

What is your Credit score?

  • Credit allows buyers to access goods and services without the use of actual money. (Meaning anywhere you swipe your card for some Mexican food or when you book a hotel night you can do that without coughing up any money in that moment while still enjoying the food and hotel service.     

  • Determines your eligibility for loans, purchasing homes, vehicles, etc (Credit score is a strong indicator for banks on whether or not they think you are suitable and responsible to maintain a loan on a home, vehicle, or even schooling.)

  • Credit also allows you to qualify for low-interest rates in a multifaceted way (When one has a good credit score they are able to indulge in making both small and large purchases at lower interest rates which means when they pay back what they have borrowed they pay less on the extra they owe to the firm.)     
How to maintain a High Score
What do I look for?
What is it?
  • High APR's (Annual percentage rate)
  • Annual membership fees (Sometimes firms have annual fees they charge you to bank with them. Avoiding this is usually best.)
  • Borrowing against credit card (cash advances) (Cash advances is the action of using your credit card to get money from an ATM. While it sounds nice it is not. After you take a cash advance it immediately accumulates interested meaning that 200 dollars you borrowed could amount to 300 or more in week or so. Yikes)
  • Hard Inquiries (commonly take place when you apply for a loan, credit card, and or mortgage.)

  • Decent late payment fees (All of us from time to time may forge to pay our bill for our credit card, and when that happens they bill us. Look for banks that charge less for late payments)

    Soft Inquiries (Typically occur when a person or company checks your credit report as part of a background check)

How do I maintain my credit score

  • Checking your credit Score (You can use the many sites available such as Experian or credit Karma. Some credit card firms offer free fiche credit scores.)     
  • Decent Credit lines (A credit line is the maximum at which you can spend with the credit card.)   

  • Paying on time 
  • Maintaining low utilization of your credit card. Below 10% is usually the rule of thumb. (Credit utilization is how much of the credit card line you are using. If it exceeds a certain point then you put yourself at a guaranteed risk of lower your credit score.     
  • Using no more than half of your credit line (This principle is in conjunction to the last. Sometimes staying under 10% just is not possible. But even when you find yourself over the 10% mark it is highly advised to stay below half always.)
